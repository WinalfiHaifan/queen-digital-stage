History & Legacy

From London to legend.

Follow Queen’s journey from their formation in 1970 to the performances, songs, and cultural influence that defined their legacy.

Timeline

Queen Through The Years

Follow Queen's evolution from a London rock band into a global musical legend.

1970

The Beginning

Queen was formed in London in 1970. The band began with Freddie Mercury, Brian May, and Roger Taylor, later joined by John Deacon. Together they created a unique sound that combined powerful vocals, theatrical performances, and experimental rock.

1973

First Album

Queen released their debut album "Queen" in 1973. The album introduced their progressive rock influences and showed the band's ambition to create music beyond traditional rock formulas.

1974

First International Recognition

Through songs such as "Seven Seas Of Rhye" and "Killer Queen", Queen gained worldwide attention. Their creativity and musical diversity helped them stand apart from other rock bands of the era.

1975

Bohemian Rhapsody

Queen reached global fame with "Bohemian Rhapsody" from the album "A Night At The Opera". The song combined rock, opera, and ballad elements, becoming one of the most influential songs in music history.

1977

The Stadium Era

With the release of "News Of The World", Queen created legendary anthems such as "We Will Rock You" and "We Are The Champions". These songs became symbols of celebration and sporting events worldwide.

1985

Live Aid Performance

Queen delivered one of the greatest live performances in music history at Live Aid in Wembley Stadium. Freddie Mercury's connection with the audience became an unforgettable cultural moment.

1986

The Magic Tour

The Magic Tour became Queen's final major concert tour with Freddie Mercury. The Wembley Stadium performances remain among the band's most iconic live shows.

1991

Freddie Mercury Legacy

Freddie Mercury passed away in 1991, but his influence continued through Queen's timeless music. His voice, songwriting, and performances remain an inspiration for generations.

Today

A Timeless Legacy

Today, Queen remains one of the most influential bands in history. Their songs, performances, and artistic vision continue to connect with millions of fans around the world.
